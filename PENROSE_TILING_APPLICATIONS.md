- Aperiodic + self-similar (inflation rules based on golden ratio)

### 4. Hierarchical / Multi-Scale Representations
- Different scales of the tiling handle different temporal or spatial resolutions.

### 5. Topological + Geometric Hybrid Memory
- Combine with 3-torus protection for critical long-term structures.
- Tiling handles fast compressible storage.

### 6. Planning and Counterfactuals (with Gap Backwards)
- When running the Feigenbaum gap backwards, the tiling provides geometric priors for plausible reconstructions.
- φ-distance bias favors reconstructions consistent with existing memory geometry.

### 7. Edge-of-Chaos Curriculum
- Use tiling statistics (local density, bond strength distribution) as signals for when the system is near critical regimes.
- Guide gap width tuning during training or inference.

## Why This Geometry Works Well

The golden ratio provides optimal self-similarity, compression, and resistance to perturbation (KAM stability). These are exactly the properties needed for robust, long-lived, multi-scale memory and controlled chaotic dynamics.
